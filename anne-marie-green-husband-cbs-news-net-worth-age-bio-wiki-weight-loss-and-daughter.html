    Anne Marie Green CBS News

    Marie started her career as a reporter for CKVR-TV in Barrie and at Rogers Cable News in Mississauga, both located in Ontario. In June 2001, she was a news anchor in Toronto beginning with CITY-TV. The same year, she also anchored at CablePulse 24, a 24-hour cable news channel servicing the greater Toronto area.

    Marie joined KYW-TV (CBS 3) as a general assignment reporter and also a co-anchor for Sunday morning newscasts opposite Ben Simoneau in October 2004.

    She became a substitute anchor for CBS News’ Up to the Minute in October 2012. She was consequently called the anchor for the CBS News early morning news broadcast CBS Morning News from New York City effective January 21, 2013.

    Early, Marie served as a general assignment reporter at CBS Philadelphia. She reported for both The CW Philly and CBS Philly and co-anchored CBS Philly’s weekend morning newscasts.

    While at CBS Philadelphia and The CW Philly, Green covered several national and local stories including Super Bowl XXXIX, the Amish school shooting in 2006 in the Nickel Mines, Pennsylvania, community, the 2007 Minneapolis bridge collapse, and the 2008 and 2009 World Series.

    Anne Marie Green Awards

    Marie was given the Doris Haire Award in 2007 for her reporting on the growing concern over the high number of C-sections being played in New Jersey and a Mid-Atlantic Emmy Award in 2011 for coverage of a fatal duck boat crash in the Schuylkill River.
