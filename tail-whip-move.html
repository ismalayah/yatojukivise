    From Bulbapedia, the community-driven Pokémon encyclopedia.

    Tail Whip (Japanese: しっぽをふる Tail Wag) is a non-damaging Normal-type move introduced in Generation I.

    Effect

    Tail Whip decreases the Defense stat of all adjacent opponents by one stage.

    Generations I to II

    In the Generation I and II core series games, when an in-game opponent outside of the Battle Tower uses Tail Whip, the move has a 25% chance to fail.

    Generation III onwards

    Tail Whip can be used as the second move of a Pokémon Contest combination, with the user gaining 2 bonus appeal points if Charm was used in the previous turn.

    If powered up by a Normalium Z into Z-Tail Whip, the user's Attack stat rises one stage.

    Pokémon Mystery Dungeon series

    Tail Whip lowers the enemy's Defense by one stage. This move is affected by Magic Coat.

    Pokémon Rumble Rush

    In Pokémon Rumble Rush, all status moves including Tail Whip could only be used by opposing Pokémon and could not be obtained on any caught Pokémon. Tail Whip could be seen as a move used by Boss Pikachu, Vulpix and Ponyta.

    Trivia

    • Multiple Pokémon (e.g., Flareon, Marowak and Tauros) have been capable of learning both this move and Leer at the same time in the past, despite them being functionally the same move. With the exception of Morpeko, all of these Pokémon would eventually only learn Tail Whip, even if they only had a very short tail (such as Rhyhorn) or no tail at all (such as Snubbull).
